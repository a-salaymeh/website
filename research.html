Research

My research sits at the intersection of machine learning, data science, and intelligent systems — developing methods that are both theoretically grounded and practically impactful.

A central thread of my work is multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL), where I study how networks of AI agents learn to cooperate and compete to discover optimal strategies. I apply these frameworks to intelligent transportation systems, exploring how vehicles and infrastructure can coordinate to reduce congestion, improve safety, and enhance efficiency.

Alongside this, I pursue applied work in deep learning and data science, transforming raw data into actionable insights across business analytics, healthcare, and related domains.

Research Areas

Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning

Designing cooperative and competitive multi-agent systems that learn through interaction, with a focus on scalable algorithms and safe exploration.

Intelligent Transportation Systems

Applying AI to traffic signal control, autonomous vehicle coordination, and route optimization to improve flow and safety.

Deep Learning

Building neural network architectures for perception, prediction, and generation across vision, time-series, and language tasks.

Data Science & Analytics

Statistical modeling, feature engineering, and visualization applied to large, heterogeneous datasets with an emphasis on interpretability.

Business Analytics

Leveraging ML and statistical methods to support organizational decision-making, forecasting, and risk analysis.

Data Mining

Discovering patterns, clusters, and associations in structured and unstructured data through scalable algorithms.
