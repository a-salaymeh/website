Welcome

I am an Assistant Professor of Information Technology at Lawrence Technological University, where I teach and conduct research in Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, Reinforcement Learning, and data-driven methods, with applications in Intelligent Transportation, smart communities, and business analytics.

I enjoy working at the intersection of research and real-world applications. My research combines graph machine learning, multi-agent reinforcement learning, and large-scale data analytics to address complex problems with meaningful societal impact. I also collaborate with industry and government partners to translate research into practical solutions.

I lead the MIND Lab (Machine Intelligence and Data Laboratory), where graduate and undergraduate students work on real-world problems in intelligent transportation systems, mobility analytics, and AI-driven decision-making. Current projects explore the use of graph neural networks and multi-agent reinforcement learning on real transportation datasets, from algorithm development to prototype implementation and evaluation.

As an educator, I believe the best learning happens when theory meets practice. I aim to create engaging course experiences that prepare students to think critically and tackle real problems. Through mentoring undergraduate and graduate students in the lab and in the classroom, I enjoy building curiosity and a genuine interest in research. Students in the MIND Lab regularly publish, present at conferences, and collaborate with external partners.

If you are a prospective student interested in joining the MIND Lab, or a researcher or industry partner interested in collaboration, I would be happy to hear from you.
