Welcome

I am an Assistant Professor of Information Technology at Lawrence Technological University. My teaching and research both center on machine learning, reinforcement learning, and data-driven methods — and how those tools can be applied to problems in intelligent transportation, smart communities, and business analytics.

Before joining LTU, I spent eight years at Wayne State University as a lecturer and graduate academic advisor in Computer Science and Data Science & Business Analytics. That background shapes how I teach: I work to connect course material to the problems students will actually face in practice.

My current research focuses on multi-agent reinforcement learning for traffic systems and on applying graph neural networks and predictive modeling to real mobility datasets. I work with graduate students in the MIND Lab on projects that run from algorithm design through to working prototypes.

If you are a prospective student interested in joining the lab, or a collaborator with a problem that fits this space, feel free to reach out.
