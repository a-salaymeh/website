About

About Me

I am an Assistant Professor of Information Technology in the College of Business and Information Technology at Lawrence Technological University. My research lies at the intersection of machine learning, data science, and intelligent systems — with a particular focus on how AI can be applied to real-world problems in transportation, business analytics, and decision-making.

Before joining LTU, I spent over eight years at Wayne State University, where I served as a lecturer and graduate academic advisor in the Computer Science department, supporting students across the Computer Science and Data Science & Business Analytics programs in setting academic and career goals.

I am passionate about bridging rigorous academic research with practical impact, and I welcome collaboration with students and colleagues who share an interest in AI, machine learning, and data-driven solutions.

Research

Research Interests

Machine Learning

Developing and applying ML algorithms to complex, real-world datasets across transportation, business, and healthcare domains.

Data Science

Extracting actionable insights from large-scale data through statistical modeling, visualization, and predictive analytics.

Deep Learning

Designing deep neural network architectures for pattern recognition, classification, and sequence modeling tasks.

Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning

Building cooperative and competitive agent systems that learn optimal policies through environmental interaction.

Intelligent Transportation Systems

Applying AI and data-driven approaches to improve traffic flow, safety, and autonomous vehicle coordination.

Business Analytics

Leveraging data analytics and ML to support informed business decision-making and strategic planning.
