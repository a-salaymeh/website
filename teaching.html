Philosophy

Teaching Philosophy

My approach to teaching is rooted in connecting foundational concepts to real-world applications. I believe that students learn best when they can see how abstract theory translates into practical tools that address meaningful problems. In every course I teach, I strive to build that bridge.

Drawing on my experience advising graduate students at Wayne State University, I also place strong emphasis on helping students develop the professional skills — critical thinking, communication, and problem-solving — that complement their technical education and prepare them for careers in industry and academia.

Current Institution

Courses at LTU

Introduction to Machine Learning

Foundational concepts in supervised and unsupervised learning, model evaluation, and feature engineering. Covers linear models, decision trees, ensembles, and neural network basics. Hands-on Python labs with scikit-learn and real datasets.

Data Science for Business

Introduces data science methods tailored for business decision-making. Topics include data wrangling, exploratory analysis, predictive modeling, and communicating results to non-technical stakeholders.

Data Mining Algorithms

In-depth study of algorithms for pattern recognition, clustering, classification, and association rule learning. Covers both classical and modern approaches, with a focus on scalability and interpretability.

Visual Analytics

Principles of data visualization and human-computer interaction for analytical reasoning. Students learn to design effective dashboards and interactive visualizations using tools such as Tableau and Python (Matplotlib, Plotly, Seaborn).

Database Management Systems

Relational database design, SQL, normalization, transactions, and indexing. Introduces NoSQL paradigms and modern cloud database services. Students complete a semester-long database design project.

Students

Student Advising

I am available to advise undergraduate and graduate students interested in pursuing research in machine learning, data science, and intelligent systems. I particularly encourage students from underrepresented backgrounds to reach out.

If you are a graduate student looking for a thesis or dissertation advisor, please send me an email with your CV, unofficial transcript, and a brief paragraph describing your research interests.
