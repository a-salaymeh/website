Teaching

My approach to teaching connects foundational concepts to real-world applications, helping students see how theory becomes practical tools for meaningful problems.

Drawing on my experience advising graduate students, I emphasize the professional skills — critical thinking, communication, and problem-solving — that complement technical education and prepare students for careers in industry and academia.

Courses

Introduction to Machine Learning

Supervised and unsupervised learning, model evaluation, and feature engineering. Hands-on Python labs with scikit-learn on real datasets.

Data Science for Business

Data science methods for business decision-making: wrangling, exploratory analysis, predictive modeling, and communicating results.

Data Mining Algorithms

Algorithms for pattern recognition, clustering, classification, and association rule learning, with a focus on scalability.

Visual Analytics

Principles of data visualization for analytical reasoning. Designing dashboards and interactive visualizations with modern tools.

Database Management Systems

Relational design, SQL, normalization, transactions, and indexing, with an introduction to NoSQL and cloud databases.

Student Advising

I am available to advise undergraduate and graduate students interested in machine learning, data science, and intelligent systems. I especially encourage students from underrepresented backgrounds to reach out.

If you are looking for a thesis or dissertation advisor, please email me with your CV, unofficial transcript, and a brief paragraph describing your research interests.
